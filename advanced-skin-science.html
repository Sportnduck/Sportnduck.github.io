Advanced Skin Science

Be your own kind of beautiful.

Advanced skincare ingredients can offer powerful support for skin health, texture, and tone when used thoughtfully. This guide introduces some of the most effective science-based ingredient categories used in modern skincare.

Peptides

Peptides are small chains of amino acids that act as signals in the skin. They can support collagen production, skin repair, and improved elasticity. Certain copper peptides may also help support skin regeneration.

Retinoids

Retinoids are vitamin A derivatives known for improving skin texture, tone, and collagen production. They can help with fine lines, acne, and uneven pigmentation but should be introduced gradually.

Barrier Repair

Healthy skin begins with a strong barrier. Ceramides, fatty acids, and cholesterol help reinforce the skin’s protective structure and support hydration.

Brightening Systems

Ingredients such as Vitamin C, Azelaic Acid, and Tranexamic Acid help improve the appearance of uneven skin tone and pigmentation.

LED Therapy

Different LED wavelengths may support different skin goals. Red light may help support collagen and repair, blue light may help with acne support, and yellow light may help calm redness.

Advanced Technologies

Newer skincare technologies include growth factors, exosomes, and bioengineered ingredients designed to support skin regeneration and communication between cells.

Advanced ingredients are powerful tools, but the best routine depends on your skin’s individual needs.
